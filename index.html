咱人 · THE HOKKIEN HERITAGE PROJECT

One language, a thousand ports.

Your family may have left southern Fujian generations ago — for Manila, Penang, Medan, Singapore, Cebu, or ports even farther away. But the words never left. They remain in the food on your table, the names your grandparents used, the gods beside the family altar, and the stories nobody thought to write down. Welcome to the worldwide home of Hokkien heritage.

音 · 鄉音未改

You may already speak more Hokkien than you think.

Words such as lumpia, popiah, bihon, misua, tikoy, and kway teow crossed the sea with families from Quanzhou and Zhangzhou. They survived across generations, often without a single formal lesson. This site helps you see where those words came from — and how each port made them its own.

lumpia 潤餅 lūn-piáⁿ popiah 薄餅 po̍h-piáⁿ bihon 米粉 bí-hún misua 麵線 mī-sòaⁿ tikoy 甜粿 tiⁿ-kóe bak kut teh 肉骨茶 kway teow 粿條 kóe-tiâu hopia 好餅 hó-piáⁿ

No single port owns the language. Penang, Manila, Singapore, Medan, Quanzhou, and Zhangzhou each kept a different part of its history.
